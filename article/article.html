YOASOBI

Yoasobi (stylized in all caps) is a Japanese music duo composed of vocaloid music producer Ayase and vocalist Ikura. The group has released the songs based on short stories posted on Monogatary, a website operated by Sony Music Entertainment Japan. Now, the group also has released songs from various novels and novels newly written in tie-ups.

Both members of Yoasobi had active music careers prior to forming the group. Ayase gained a following on the video hosting site Niconico since his first upload on December 24, 2018, a song titled "Sentensei Assault Girl" (先天性アサルトガール). On November 23, 2019, he released an EP, Yūrei Tōkyō (幽霊東京), which reached third on iTunes' album chart. Lilas Ikuta (幾田りら), performing under the name Ikura, had already released two EPs: Rerise in 2017 and Jukebox in 2019.

The first song released by the duo was "Yoru ni Kakeru", based on Mayo Hoshino's short story Thanatos no Yūwaku hosted on Monogatary. The music video was released first on November 16, 2019, where it broke 10 million views in five months on YouTube as well as being popular on TikTok. It was subsequently released as a single on December 15, 2019, where it topped the popularity charts on both Spotify and Line Music. More than five months after its release, the single would top the Billboard Japan Hot 100 on June 1, 2020.

Following the success of their first release, the duo released their second single, "Ano Yume o Nazotte" on January 18, 2020. The song based on Sōta Ishiki's Yume no Shizuku to Hoshi no Hana. On April 20, the duo released the teaser for their third single, "Halzion", which is based on Shunki Hashizume's Soredemo, Happy End. "Halzion" marked the first time that Yoasobi collaborated with a professional novelist, whereas the first two were amateur writers. The single was released as a promotional project by Suntory to advertise their new energy drink named Zone. The single was officially released on May 11.

On January 6, 2021, Yoasobi released the first extended play and first physical release The Book, included the 6 singles and 3 new tracks, "Encore", "Epilogue" as intro, and "Prologue" as outro. The duo also released the seventh single, "Kaibutsu" in the same day. The song has been appointed as the opening theme song for the second season of the anime television series Beastars. The anime's ending theme song and the duo's eight single "Yasashii Suisei", was released on January 20, 2021. The duo opened the fanclub website "Club Yoasobi" (CLUB 夜遊) on January 20,[18] and annouced the duo will hold their first concert, Keep Out Theater on February 14. The duo will release the first physical single include both "Kaibutsu" and "Yasashii Suisei" as a double A-side on March 24, 2021.
